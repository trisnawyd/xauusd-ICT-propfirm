1. What It Is
Weekly Initial Jobless Claims counts first-time unemployment benefit
applications filed in the US each week. It's one of the most real-time
reads on labor market health available to markets. Investors treat it as
a leading indicator — rising claims flag hiring slowdowns before other
data confirms them.

2. Market Impact (Pre-Release Scenario Analysis)
The forecast of 205K represents a +16K jump from the strong 189K prior
reading. Three scenarios:

IF actual ≈ forecast (203–207K) — in-line:
  - Stocks:     Mild negative (labor softening = growth concern)
  - Bonds:      Slightly bullish (yields tick lower, rate cut odds tick up)
  - USD:        Slightly weaker
  - Gold (XAU): Mildly bullish — USD softness supports gold

IF actual BEATS (< 198K — labor still tight):
  - Stocks:     Mixed to negative (rate hike fears return)
  - Bonds:      Bearish (yields rise, cut hopes fade)
  - USD:        Strengthens notably
  - Gold (XAU): BEARISH — USD rally pushes XAU lower; expect quick spike down

IF actual MISSES (> 212K — labor weakening):
  - Stocks:     Initial drop, then possible recovery (soft landing narrative)
  - Bonds:      Bullish (yields drop fast, rate cuts priced in)
  - USD:        Weakens sharply
  - Gold (XAU): BULLISH — biggest reaction; XAU could spike 20–40 pips

3. Historical Context
Claims have been running in the 180–193K range for several weeks —
historically tight. The forecast jump to 205K, if confirmed, would be
the sharpest single-week rise in recent months and could mark an early
inflection in the US labor cycle. However, one weekly reading is
insufficient to call a trend change; markets will want to see follow-
through in subsequent weeks before repricing the Fed path materially.
