1. What It Is
ADP Non-Farm Employment Change measures monthly change in US private-sector payrolls from ADP's payroll processor — covering ~700,000 businesses. It is the primary leading indicator for Friday's official NFP report. Investors and traders use it to gauge US labor market momentum and to front-run USD direction before the official release.

2. Market Impact
- Stocks: Slightly negative — stronger-than-previous employment (118K vs 109K) reduces rate-cut urgency, pressuring equity valuations higher
- Bonds: Bearish (yields up) — resilient hiring supports Fed "higher for longer"; 10Y yields likely to tick up
- Currency (USD): BULLISH — beat vs previous strengthens USD and rate-hold narrative
- Gold (XAU/USD): BEARISH — USD strength headwind; reduced rate-cut expectations weigh on non-yielding gold

3. Historical Context
ADP has been printing 100–150K over recent months, reflecting a moderating but still-solid US labor market. This 118K reading (above 109K previous) signals a mild re-acceleration in private hiring. It reinforces the narrative that the Fed has no immediate urgency to cut rates, which is structurally bearish for gold.
