### 1. What It Is

**Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP)** measures how many jobs were added or lost in the U.S. economy, excluding farm workers and a few other categories. It is one of the most important economic reports because it gives a quick read on labor market strength and can influence Federal Reserve interest-rate decisions.

### 2. Market Impact

_Assuming the forecast is 95K and actual data is not yet available:_

- **Stocks:** Stronger-than-95K → bullish for growth-sensitive sectors; much weaker-than-95K → recession concerns may weigh on stocks.
    
- **Bonds:** Stronger-than-expected → bond prices likely fall (yields rise); weaker-than-expected → bond prices likely rise (yields fall).
    
- **Currency (USD):** Stronger-than-expected → USD typically strengthens; weaker-than-expected → USD typically weakens.
    
- **Gold:** Strong NFP → often bearish for gold; weak NFP → often bullish as rate-cut expectations increase.
    

### 3. Historical Context

The trend has been gradually slowing, with the previous reading at **115K** and the forecast at **95K**. This suggests a cooling labor market, though job growth remains positive. Investors are watching for signs that employment is weakening enough to support future Fed rate cuts.
