1. **What It Is**  
   The Nonfarm Payrolls report measures monthly US job changes outside farming. It’s a top-tier gauge of economic health, directly shaping Federal Reserve rate expectations and broad investor sentiment.

2. **Market Impact** (based on actual vs. 95K forecast)  
   - **Stocks:** A beat (>95K) lifts equities on growth strength, but an oversized surprise may spark rate-hike jitters. A miss (<95K) weighs on stocks via recession fears.  
   - **Bonds:** Strong payrolls push yields up (bond prices fall) as rate-cut bets fade; a weak print sparks a rally, lowering yields.  
   - **Currency:** The US dollar rallies on an upside surprise (hawkish Fed), and falls sharply on a downside miss.  
   - **Gold:** A strong report pressures gold through higher real yields and a stronger dollar; a weak print boosts gold as a safe haven.

3. **Historical Context**  
   Payrolls have been decelerating from post-pandemic peaks; the prior 115K was already soft. A further drop toward 95K would mark a clear cooling trend, and a sub-100K print would be a notable inflection point, heightening labor-market slowdown concerns.
