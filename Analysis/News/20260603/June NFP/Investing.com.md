### Jobs Scorecard: What It Is
Nonfarm Payrolls measures how many jobs were created or lost in the U.S. economy (excluding farms). It’s a top-tier economic indicator—strong job growth suggests a healthy economy, while weak numbers can stoke recession fears.

### Market Impact: Who Moves, and Why?
- **Stocks:** If the actual number comes in below forecast, stocks typically fall—slower hiring hints at economic slowdown.
- **Bonds:** Weak payrolls often push bond prices up (yields down) as investors anticipate potential Fed rate cuts.
- **Currency (USD):** A disappointing jobs figure usually weakens the dollar, as lower rates become more likely.
- **Commodities:** Gold may gain if the report triggers risk-off sentiment.

### Historical Context
Nonfarm Payrolls have been trending lower in recent months (115K prior, now forecasted at 95K), signaling a cooling labor market. This deceleration, if confirmed by the actual release, would mark a continued slowdown from earlier robust post-pandemic job growth. Investors will be watching for signs of a possible inflection point.
