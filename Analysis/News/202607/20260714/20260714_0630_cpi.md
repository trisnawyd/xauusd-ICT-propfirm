## 3. Post-Mortem — Two Errors

### Error 1 — Release time was off by three hours (systemic bug)

The pre-release file stated CPI "fires **15:30 UTC**" and set an avoid-window of
**15:15–15:45 UTC**. US CPI is always **08:30 ET = 12:30 UTC**.

**Root cause:** `get_ohlcv` returns candle timestamps in **broker time (Exness = UTC+3)**,
not UTC. The `15:30` stamp on the release candle was read off the broker clock and written
down as UTC. Cross-check that catches it every time: `get_session_levels` reports true UTC
(`UTC:12:33`) while the same-moment M1 candle is stamped `15:33`.

**Consequence:** the file's "operator decision required before 15:15 UTC" deadline pointed
at a moment nearly three hours *after* the event had already blown through the book.
Followed literally, it would have had the operator reviewing news risk long after the news.

→ Rule added to `CLAUDE.md` (Broker Time vs UTC) so this cannot recur.

### Error 2 — The directional asymmetry was backwards

The pre-release thesis was:

> *"A -0.1% m/m forecast is a low bar to beat… The market is positioned for soft;
> the pain trade is hot. This aligns with the standing **BEARISH** HTF bias."*

It argued the surprise risk skewed **hawkish** (hot core → gold down through 3983.20).
The actual print was the cleanest possible **dovish** shock, and gold went vertical.

**Root cause — this is the lesson, not the bad luck:** the reasoning ran *backwards from
the bias.* A BEARISH HTF map was already standing, a with-trend short was already resting
at 4042.5, and the news note then constructed an asymmetry argument that made the news
risk "skewed *in our favour*." That is thesis-defence dressed as analysis. The honest
pre-release call on a HIGH-impact two-way event with a coiled nine-hour base was
**NEUTRAL with symmetric scenarios and no lean** — which is exactly what the
`xauusd_implication: NEUTRAL` field said, before the prose talked itself out of it.

## 4. What It Got Right

- **Core, not headline, was correctly named as the swing factor.** The disinflation-optics

- The scenario map was complete. The error was in assigning confidence to a branch, not in
  failing to map the branches.
