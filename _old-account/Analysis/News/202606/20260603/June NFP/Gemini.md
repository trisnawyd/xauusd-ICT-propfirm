### 1. What It Is

United States Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) measures the net number of jobs created or lost nationwide monthly, excluding agricultural, private household, and non-profit workers. It serves as the definitive health check on economic momentum. Investors track it obsessively because strong hiring fuels consumer spending, corporate earnings, and Federal Reserve interest rate decisions.

### 2. Market Impact

If the upcoming actual reading prints a standard response to your forecast:

- **Stocks:** Potentially negative. A sharp drop to 95K confirms economic deceleration, risking corporate revenue stagnation and sparking recession worries.
    
- **Bonds:** Positive. Slower job growth suggests cooling inflation, pushing bond yields down and Treasury bond prices up on anticipation of faster Fed rate cuts.
    
- **Currency:** Negative. Weak job growth reduces the appeal of the U.S. Dollar (USD) as yields fall, strengthening competing currencies.
    

### 3. Historical Context

The U.S. labor market has shown signs of structural moderation recently. The drop from previous hiring clips to April's 115K reading highlights a clear cooling trend. A print matching the 95K forecast would mark a critical transition into a tighter, more restrictive economic phase.
